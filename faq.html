Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Outsourced CFO?

An Outsourced CFO is a part-time or outsourced Chief Financial Officer who provides the same strategic financial leadership as a full-time CFO, without the full-time cost.

How is an Outsourced CFO different from a bookkeeper or accountant?

Bookkeepers and accountants focus on recording transactions and compliance. An Outsourced CFO goes further, providing financial strategy, forecasting, cash flow management, and decision-making support to help your business grow.

What types of businesses benefit from an Outsourced CFO?

Startups, growing companies, investor-backed businesses, nonprofits, and small to mid-sized organizations that need financial strategy but don't require a full-time CFO.

How does pricing work for Outsourced CFO services?

We tailor our services based on your needs. Pricing is typically structured as a monthly retainer or project-based fee, depending on the scope and engagement level.

How much time will an Outsourced CFO spend with my business?

That depends on your needs. Some businesses need just a few hours per week, while others benefit from deeper involvement during critical growth or transition phases.

Can an Outsourced CFO help with fundraising or securing financing?

Yes. We can help prepare financial models, pitch decks, and reporting to give investors or lenders confidence in your business.

Do I still need an accountant or CPA if I hire an Outsourced CFO?

Yes. An Outsourced CFO complements, not replaces your accounting team. We focus on strategy, financial planning, and decision support, while your CPA or accountant handles tax filings and compliance.

How quickly can an Outsourced CFO start making an impact?

Often within the first 30–60 days. We begin by reviewing your financials, identifying immediate priorities (like cash flow visibility or reporting improvements), and implementing quick wins while building a longer-term strategy.
